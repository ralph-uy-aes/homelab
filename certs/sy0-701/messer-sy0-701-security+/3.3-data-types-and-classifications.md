# 3.3 Data Types and Classifications

#### Data Types
- Regulated
    - Managed by a third party
    - Government laws and statutes
- Trade Secret
    - An organization's secret formulas
    - Often unique to an organization
- Intellectual Property
    - May be publicly visible
    - Copyright and trademark restrictions
- Legal Information
    - Court records and documents, judge and attorney information, etc
    - PII, other sensitive data
    - Usually stored in many different systems
- Financial Information
    - Internal company financial details
    - Customer finances
    - Payment records
    - Credit cards, bank accounts,etc
- Human Readable
    - Humans can interpret and read data, based usually on some natural language
- Non-human readable
    - Hard to interpret, usually meant for machines
    - Encoded data, barcodes, QRs, etc
- Some are hybrid formats
    - CSV, XML, JSON, etc

#### Classifying Sensitive Data
- Not all data is equally sensitive
- Different levels require different security and handling
    - Additional permissions
    - Different process to view
    - Restricted network access

#### Data Classifications
- Proprietary
    - Data is the property of an organization
    - May include trade secrets
    - Unique to the organization
- PII - Personally Identifiable Information
    - Data that can be used to identify an individual
    - Name, DOB, possible some answers to secret questions, biometrics
- PHI - Protected Health Information
    - Health information associated with an individual
    - Health status, health care records, payments, etc
- Sensitive
    - Intellectual Property (IP), PII, PHI
- Confidential
    - Very sensitive, must be approved to view
- Public/Unclassified
    - Anyone can view
- Private/Classified/Restricted
    - Restricted, may need NDA to see
- Critical
    - Data that should always be available
    - Preserve its uptime
