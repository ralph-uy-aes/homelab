# 4.2 Asset Management

#### Acquisition/Procurement Process
- The corporate process before buying a new good or service
- Usually comes from a request from a user, possibly in another department
    - User works with IT and finance
    - Requires a lot of formal approvals and budgeting information
- Negotiate with supplier
    - Then purchase, invoice, payment

#### Assignment/Accounting
- A central asset tracking system
    - Used by different parts of the organization
- Ownership
    - Associate a person with an asset they own
    - Useful for tracking
- Classify
    - Based on type of asset
    - Hardware (capital expenditure - degrades due to wear, taxed differently)
    - Software (operating expenditure - doesn't degrade, also taxed differently)

#### Monitoring/Asset Tracking
- Inventory every asset, goods and service alike
- Associate a support ticket with a device make and model
- Enumeration
    - List all parts of an asset
    - Inside of a PC, list all components and peripherals
- Add an asset tag
    - Barcode, RFID, visible tracking number, organization/department name

#### Media Sanitization
- Required process for disposal or decommissioning
- Different use cases
    - Clean a hard drive for future use
    - Permanent deletion of data 
    - Destruction

#### Physical Destruction
- Shredder/Pulverizer
- Drill/Hammer
- Electromagnetic (degaussing)
    - Removes magnetic field
- Incineration

#### Certificate of Destruction
- Required for destruction done with a 3rd party
- Need to confirm that your data is destroyed, so 3rd party creates a certificate
- A paper trail of broken data

#### Data Retention
- Backup your data
- Regulatory Compliance
    - A certain amount of data backup may be required
    - Some organizations are required to keep emails for a certain number of years
- Operational Needs
    - In case of accidental deletion or disasters
- Differentiate retained data by type and application
    - For easier recovery
