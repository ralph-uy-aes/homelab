# 2.4 Wireless Attacks

#### Starts as a normal day
- Surfing and suddenly tour wifi is down
- Keeps going down even when you reconnect
- This could be a wireless deauthentication attack
    - Significant wireless DoS Attack

#### 802.11 management frames
- 802.11 wireless includes a number of management frames
    - Frames that make everything work
- Important for the operation of 802.11 wireless
    - How to find APs, manage QoS, associate/dissociate with an AP
- Original wireless standards did not add protection for management frames
    - Sent in the clear, no authentication or validation

#### Protecting against deauth attacks
- IEEE has already addressed the problem
    - Updates included with 802.11ac
- Some important management frames are encrypted
    - Disassociate, deauthenticate, channel switch announcements, etc
- Not everything is encrypted
    - Beacons, probes, authentication, association

#### RF Jamming
- Denial of service
    - Prevent wireless communication
- Transmit interfering wireless signals
    - Decrease signal-to-noise ratio at the receiving device
    - Receiving device can't hear the good signal
- Sometimes unintentional
    - Microwave ovens, fluorescent lights, etc..

#### Wireless Jamming
- Many different types
    - Constant, random bits/ Constant, legitimate frames
    - Cause noise and issues
    - Send at random times to make troubleshooting harder
    - Reactive jamming, only activates when something tries to communicate
- Need ot be somewhere close
- Fox hunting
    - You'll need the right equipment to hunt down the jame
    - Directional antenna, attenuator
