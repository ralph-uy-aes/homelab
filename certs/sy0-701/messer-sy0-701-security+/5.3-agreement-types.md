# 5.3 Agreement Types

#### Common Agreements
- Service Level Agreements (SLA)
    - Minimum terms for services provided
    - Uptime, response time agreement, etc
    - Commonly used between customers and service providers
- Ex. Contract with ISP
    - SLA is no more than four hours of unscheduled downtime
    - Technician will be dispatched if so
    - May require customer to keep spare equipment on-site
- Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)
    - Broad goals of what multiple organizations will accomplish working together
    - May include statements of confidentiality
    - Informal letter of intent, not a signed contract
- Memorandum of Agreement (MOA)
    - One step above MOU, more details
    - Can have legally binding things
    - Both sides conditionally agree to the objectives
- Master Service Agreement (MSA)
    - Legal contract and agreement of terms
    - Broad framework to cover later transactions
    - Many detailed negotiations happen here
    - Future projects will be based on this agreement
- Work Order (WO)/Statement of Work (SOW)
    - Specific list of items to be completed
    - Used in conjunction with MSA
    - Details the scope of the job, location, deliverables schedule, acceptance criteria, and more
    - Was the job done properly? Let's refer to the SOW
- Business Partners Agreement (BPA)
    - Going into business together
    - Owner stake
    - Financial contract
    - Decision-making
        - Who makes decisions
        - BPA lists specific individuals and scope
    - Contingencies
        - Financial issues
        - Disaster recovery

#### Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Confidentiality agreement between parties
    - Information in agreement should be disclosed
- Protects confidential information
    - Trade secrets
    - Business activities
- Unilateral
    - Only one party cannot disclose
- Bilateral
    - All parties cannot disclose
- Multilateral
    - More than one or all parties cannot disclose
- Formal contract
    - Signed before discussing information to be non-disclosed
