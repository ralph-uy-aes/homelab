# 4.8 Digital Forensics

#### Digital Forensics
- Collecting and protecting data regarding an intrusion is important in forensics
- RFC 3227 - Guidelines for Evidence Collection and Archiving
    - Has best practices
- Standard digital forensic process
    - Acquisition, Analysis, and Reporting
- Must be detail oriented

#### Legal Hold
- Legal technique to preserve relevant information
    - Prepare for impending litigation
    - Initiated by legal counsel
- Hold notification
    - Custodians are instructed to preserve data
- Separate repository for electronically stored information (ESI)
    - Many different data sources and types
    - Unique workflow and retention requirements
- Ongoing preservation
    - You are responsible for keeping data safe and available when the court needs it

#### Chain of Custody
- Control evidence
    - Maintain integrity of data
- Everyone who contacts the device must be in the chain of custody
    - Use hashes and digital signatures
    - Avoid data tampering
- Label and catalog everything
    - Digitally tag all items for ongoing documentation
    - Seal and store

#### Acquisition
- Obtain data
    - Many different sources
- Some data is in more than one affected system
    - Firewalls, network, servers, etc
- Virtual systems
    - Get a snapshot
- Look for any other digital items
    - Artifacts
    - Log information, recycle bins, bookmarks, saved logins, etc

#### Reporting
- Document the findings
    - For internal use, legal proceedings
- Summarize information
    - Overview of event
- Detailed explanation of data acquisition
    - Step-by-step method of the process
- Findings
    - An analysis of the data
- Conclusion
    - Professional results, given the analysis

#### Preservation
- Handling the evidence
    - Isolate and protect the data
    - Analyze unaltered data
- Manage the collection process
    - Work from copies, not the real systems
    - Manage data collection from mobile devices
- Live collection has become an important skill
    - Data may be encrypted or difficult to collect after powering down
- Follow best practices to ensure admissibility in court

#### E-Discovery
- Process of collecting, preparing, reviewing, interpreting, and producing electronic documents
- E-Discovery gathers data required by the legal process
    - Does not generally involve analysis
    - No consideration of intent
- Works together with digital forensics
    - E-discovery process obtains a storage drive
    - Data on drive is smaller than expected
    - Forensics experts determines that data was deleted and attempt to recover data
