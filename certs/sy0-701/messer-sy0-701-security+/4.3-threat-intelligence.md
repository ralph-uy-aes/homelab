# 4.3 Threat Intelligence

#### Threat Intelligence
- Research threats and threat actors
    - Know your enemy
- Data is everywhere
    - Hacker group profiles, tools used by the attackers, and much more
- Make decisions based on this intelligence
    - Invest in the best prevention tools

#### OSINT
- Open-source
    - Anyone can find it
- Internet
    - Discussion groups, socmed
- Government data
    - Public reports, websites, hearings, etc
- Commercial data
    - Maps, news, financial data, databases

#### Proprietary/Third-party Intelligence
- A vendor who supplies threat intelligence can sell compiled data to you
- Threat intelligence services monitor broadly and provide analytics
    - Very helpful since they use different data sources
- Constantly monitors threats
    - Always up to date
    - Usually have automated prevention workflows

#### Information-sharing Organization
- Provide both public and private threat intelligence
- Shares critical security details
- Cyber Threat Alliance (CTA)
    - Members upload specifically formatted threat intelligence
    - CTA scores each submission and validates across other submissions
    - Other members can extract validated data

#### Dark Web Intelligence
- Dark web
    - Overlay network that uses the Internet
    - Requires specific software and configurations to access
- Hacking groups and services
    - Activities
    - Tools and techniques
    - Credit card sales
    - Accounts and passwords
- Monitor forums for activity
    - Be scared if your name shows up
