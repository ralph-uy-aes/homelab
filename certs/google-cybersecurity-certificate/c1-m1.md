# Course 1 – Module 1:Common Cybersecurity Terminology

Cybersecurity ensures the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information by protecting networks, devices, people, and data from unauthorized access or exploitation.

## Key Terms and Concepts

- **Compliance** – Adhering to internal standards and external regulations to avoid fines and breaches.
- **Security frameworks** – Guidelines for building plans to mitigate risks to data and privacy.
- **Security controls** – Safeguards designed to reduce specific security risks.
- **Security posture** – An organization’s ability to defend critical assets and react to change.
- **Threat actor** – Any person or group presenting a security risk.
- **Internal threat** – A current/former employee, vendor, or partner who poses a risk (accidental or intentional).
- **Network security** – Keeping an organization's network infrastructure secure from unauthorized access.
- **Cloud security** – Ensuring cloud assets are properly configured and access is limited to authorized users.

**Programming** creates instructions for computers to automate tasks, review web traffic, or alert on suspicious activity.

*Tip:* Use the NIST glossary or other reputable sources to stay updated on cybersecurity terms.

# Transferable and Technical Skills

## Transferable Skills

- **Communication** – Clearly convey information to technical and non-technical stakeholders.
- **Problem-solving** – Identify attack patterns and find efficient solutions, often through compromise.
- **Time management** – Prioritize urgent tasks to minimize damage and risk.
- **Growth mindset** – Remain willing to learn as the industry evolves.
- **Diverse perspectives** – Collaborate and respect different viewpoints for better solutions.

## Technical Skills

- **Programming languages** – Automate time-consuming tasks like data searching and pattern analysis.
- **SIEM tools** – Collect and analyze log data to monitor critical activities and identify threats.
- **Intrusion Detection Systems (IDSs)** – Monitor system activity and alert on possible intrusions.
- **Threat landscape knowledge** – Stay current on attacker trends, malware, and methodologies.
- **Incident response** – Follow established procedures to investigate and remediate security incidents.

# Module 1 Glossary

- **Cybersecurity (security)** – Protecting networks, devices, people, and data from unauthorized access.
- **Cloud security** – Securing cloud assets and limiting access to authorized users.
- **Internal threat** – Insider (employee, vendor, partner) posing a security risk.
- **Network security** – Protecting network infrastructure from unauthorized access.
- **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)** – Information used to infer an individual's identity.
- **Security posture** – Ability to defend assets and react to change.
- **Sensitive PII (SPII)** – A stricter subset of PII.
- **Technical skills** – Knowledge of specific tools, procedures, and policies.
- **Threat** – Any circumstance or event that can negatively impact assets.
- **Threat actor** – Any person or group presenting a security risk.
- **Transferable skills** – Skills from other areas applicable to different careers.
