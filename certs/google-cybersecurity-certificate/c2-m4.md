# Course 2 – Module 4: Incident Response Playbooks

## What Is a Playbook?

A **playbook** is a manual detailing step‑by‑step actions for operational tasks, especially incident response. It is accompanied by a **strategy** (team expectations) and a **plan** (how to complete tasks).

Playbooks are **living documents** – updated regularly due to:
- Identified failures or oversights.
- Changes in industry standards, laws, or regulations.
- Evolving threat actor tactics and techniques.

---

## Types of Playbooks

- **Incident response playbooks** – For specific incidents (ransomware, BEC, vishing, etc.).
- **Vulnerability response playbooks** – For handling identified vulnerabilities.

Both align with the organization’s **business continuity plan** (recovery after disruption). They ensure legal compliance, minimize errors, and maintain a sense of urgency.

---

## Common Steps in Incident/Vulnerability Playbooks

1. Preparation  
2. Detection  
3. Analysis  
4. Containment  
5. Eradication  
6. Recovery  
7. Post‑incident activities (lessons learned, coordination)

---

## Playbooks and Security Tools

- **SIEM tools** – Flag unusual activity; playbooks guide the analyst's response.
- **SOAR tools** – Automate repetitive tasks (e.g., auto‑blocking accounts after failed logins); analysts then follow playbooks to resolve the issue.

*Playbooks are also called runbooks.*

---

# Module 4 Glossary

- **Incident response** – Quick attempt to identify, contain, and correct a breach.
- **Playbook** – Manual detailing operational actions.
