| **5. Identity and Access Management (IAM)** | Authenticating and authorizing users; principle of least privilege. |

| **7. Security Operations** | Investigating breaches, incident management, intrusion detection, SIEM, playbooks, forensics. |

# Threats, Risks, and Vulnerabilities

- **Threat** – Any circumstance or event that can negatively impact assets (e.g., insider threats, APTs).
- **Risk** – Anything that can impact CIA of an asset. *Risk = Likelihood of a threat.* Categories: external, internal, legacy systems, multiparty, software compliance.
- **Vulnerability** – A weakness that can be exploited (e.g., ProxyLogon, ZeroLogon, Log4Shell, PetitPotam, logging failures, SSRF).

## Risk Management Strategies

- **Acceptance** – Accept risk to avoid business disruption.
- **Avoidance** – Create a plan to avoid the risk.
- **Transference** – Transfer risk to a third party.
- **Mitigation** – Reduce the impact of known risks.

Frameworks: NIST RMF, HITRUST, OWASP Top 10.

## NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) Steps

1. **Prepare** – Activities before a breach.
2. **Categorize** – Develop risk processes.
3. **Select** – Choose and customize controls.
4. **Implement** – Execute security plans.
5. **Assess** – Determine if controls work.
6. **Authorize** – Accept remaining risks.
7. **Monitor** – Continuously oversee systems.

# Module 1 Glossary

- **Assess** – Step 5 of NIST RMF: evaluate controls.
- **Authorize** – Step 6: accept accountability for risks.
- **Business continuity** – Ability to maintain operations after disasters.
- **Categorize** – Step 2: develop risk processes.
- **External threat** – Outside risk to assets.
- **Implement** – Step 4: execute plans.
- **Internal threat** – Insider risk (employee, vendor, partner).
- **Monitor** – Step 7: observe system operations.
- **Prepare** – Step 1: pre‑breach activities.
- **Ransomware** – Encrypts data for ransom.
- **Risk** – Impact on CIA triad.
- **Risk mitigation** – Procedures to reduce impact.
- **Security posture** – Ability to defend assets and react.
- **Select** – Step 3: choose and document controls.
- **Shared responsibility** – Everyone contributes to risk reduction.
- **Social engineering** – Manipulation exploiting human error.
- **Vulnerability** – Weakness exploitable by a threat.
