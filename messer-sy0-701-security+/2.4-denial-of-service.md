# 2.4 Denial of Service

#### Denial of Service
- Force a service to fail
    - Overload the service
- Take advantage of a design failure or vulnerability
    - Keep systems patched
- Cause a system to be unavailable
    - Competitive advantage
- Create a smokescreen for some other exploit
    - Precursor to a DNS spoofing attack
- Doesn't have to be complicated
    - Turn off the power is a simple way to DoS

#### Friendly DoS
- Unintentional DoSing
- Network DoS
    - Layer 2 loop without STP
- Bandwidth DoS
    - Downloading multi-gigabyte Linux distro over a DSL line
- Water line breaks above data servers
    - Get a vacuum

#### Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)
- Launch an army of computers to bring down a service
    - Use all the bandwidth or resources, traffic spikes
- Botnets
    - Thousands of computers at your command
    - Malware
    - Zeus botnet had a peak of 3.6 million PCs
    - Coordinated attacks
- Asymmetric threat
    - Attacker has fewer resources than the victim

#### DDoS reflection and amplification
- Turn your small attack into a big one
    - Often reflected off another device or service
- Increasingly common
    - Takes advantage or internet services
- Uses protocols with little auth checks
    - NTP, DNS, ICMP
