# 5.2 Risk Analysis

#### Qualitative Risk Assessment
- Identify significant risk factors
    - Ask opinions about the significance
    - Display visually with traffic light grid or similar method
- Risk Factor, Impact, Annualized Rate of Occurrence, Cost of Controls, Overall Risk

#### Quantitative Risk Assessment
- Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO)
    - Likelihood of something to occur in a rate
- Asset Value (AV)
    - Value of asset to organization
    - Cost of asset, effect on sales, potential regulatory fines, etc
- Exposure Factor (EF)
    - Percentage of value lost due to an incident
    - Losing a quarter of the value is .25
    - Losing it entirely is 1.0
- Single Loss Expectancy (SLE)
    - Monetary loss if a single event occurs
    - AV * EF
    - Laptop Stolen = $1000 (AV) * 1.0 (EF) = $1000 (SLE)
- Annual Loss Expectancy (ALE)
    - Monetary loss for an entire year
    - ARO * SLE
    - Seven laptops stolen a year = 7 (ARO) * $1000 (SLE) = $7000 (ALE)
- Business impact can be more than monetary, so we have Quant and Qual

#### Impact
- Life
    - Most important consideration
    - Make sure everyone is safe
    - Assets are replaceable, people aren't
- Property
    - Risk to buildings and assets
- Safety
    - Think OSHA
- Finance
    - Resulting financial cost

#### Likelihood and Probability
- Risk likelihood
    - Qualitative measurement of risk
    - Rare, possible, certain, etc
- Risk probability
    - Quantitative measurement of risk
    - Statistical measure
    - Based on historical performance
- Often used interchangeably

#### Risk Appetite and Tolerance
- Risk appetite
    - Broad description of risk-taking deemed acceptable
    - Amount of accepted risk before taking any action to reduce risk
- Risk appetite posture
    - Qualitative description for readiness to take risk
    - Conservative, neutral, and expansionary
- Risk tolerance
    - An acceptable variance (usually larger) from the risk appetite
- Difference
    - Risk Appetite
        - Highway's speed limit
        - Determined by government authorities
        - Limit is acceptable balance between safety and convenience
    - Risk Tolerance
        - Drivers will be ticketed when the speed limit is violated
        - Ticketing usually occurs well above posted limit
        - Tolerance can change with road conditions, weather, traffic, etc

#### Risk Register
- Every project has a plan, but also has risk
    - Identify and document risk associated with each step
    - Apply possible solutions to identified risks
    - Monitor results
- Key risk indicators
    - Identify risks that could impact the organization
- Risk owners
    - Each indicator is assigned someone to manage the risk
- Risk threshold
    - Cost of mitigation is at least equal to value gained by mitigation
