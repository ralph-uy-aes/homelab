# 5.1 Security Policies

#### Security Policy Guidelines
- What rules are you following to provide CIA?
    - Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability
- High-level strategies
- Detailed security goals
- Security policies answer the "what" and the "why"
    - Technical security controls answer the "how"

#### Information Security Policies
- A big list of security-related policies
    - Centralized resource for processes
- Compliance requirements
    - May be mandated to have and critical to an organization
- Detailed security procedures
    - What to do when...
- List of roles and responsibilities
    - Who is in charge of what
- Words and letters only
    - Up to organization to execute

#### Acceptable Use Policies (AUP)
- What is acceptable use for company assets
    - Detailed documentation
    - May be in rules of behavior
- Protects inappropriate use of assets and limits liability on organization
    - If you're told not to do something and you do it, that's your fault

#### Business Continuity
- Not everything goes according to plan
    - Disasters can cause a disruption to the norm
- We rely on our computer systems
    - Technology is pervasive
- There needs to be an alternative during disasters
    - Manual transactions
    - Paper receipts
    - Phone calls
- Must be documented and tested before a problem occurs

#### Disaster Recovery Plan
- Formal set of policies
    - Business continuity plan that applies to everyone
- Disasters are varied
    - Difficult to make all-encompassing plans
- Must be comprehensive
    - Recovery location
    - Data recovery method
    - Application restoration
    - IT team and employee availability

#### Security Incidents
- User clicks an email attachment and execute malware
    - Malware communicates with external servers
- DDoS
    - Botnet attack
- Confidential information is stolen
    - Thief wants money or it goes public

#### Incident Response Roles
- Incident response team
    - Specialized group, trained and tested
- IT Security Management
    - Corporate Support
- Compliance Officers
    - Intricate knowledge of compliance rules
- Technical staff
    - Team in the trenches
- User community

#### NIST SP800-61
- NIST Special Publication 800-61 Revision 2
- Computer Security Incident Handling Guide
- Contains the incident response lifecycle:
    - Preparation
    - Detection and Analysis
    - Containment, Eradication, and Recovery
    - Post-incident Activity

#### Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)
- Many ways to get from idea to app
    - Many moving parts
    - Customer requirements
    - Keep process on schedule
    - Stay in budget
- No best way
    - Helps to have a framework
    - Many options

#### Change Management
- Changes need a whole process
    - Even a simple firewall configuration change
- One of the most common risks in enterprise is changing things a lot
    - Having a good process reduces risks
- Have clear policies
    - Frequency, duration, install process, fallback procedures
- Sometimes extremely hard to implement
