# 5.2 Risk Management

#### Risk Identification
- Risk management helps understand potential risk
    - Identify weaknesses before they become an issue
- An important part of any organization
    - Growth brings risk
- Risk management
    - Manage potential risk
    - Qualify internal and external threats
    - Risk analysis helps plan for contingencies

#### Performing a Risk Assessment
- Not all risk requires constant evaluation
    - Might be required to always assess the amount of risk
- One-time
    - Assessment may be part of a one-time project
    - Company acquisition, new equipment installation, unique new security threats, etc
- Continuous Assessments
    - May be part of an existing process
    - Change control requires a risk assessment as part of the change

#### Ad Hoc Assessments
- Ad Hoc means "for this"
    - Used as in for this purpose only
- Specific assessments
    - For example, assessing a specific newly found attack type, ad hoc assessment to see how your organization does
- A committee is created and th risk assessment proceeds
    - Once assessment is complete, committee is disbanded
    - May not be a need to investigate this specific risk again

#### Recurring Assessments
- Evaluation occurs on standard intervals
- Internal assessment
    - Performed every 3 months at the beginning of the quarter
- Mandated risk assessment
    - Required by some organizations
    - Some legal requirements will mandate an assessment
    - PCI DSS requires annual risk assessments
