# 3.1 Infrastructure Considerations

#### Availability
- Everyone expects a system or service to be running and ready to use
    - Fundamentally important to IT 
- However, we want to balance availability with security
    - Meaning that things should only be available to the right people
- Availability is expensive, we spend a lot of money on monitoring equipment and redundancy
- Oftentimes, availability is the success metric

#### Resilience
- How much can your systems take?
- How fast can you recover?
- Based on a lot of variables
    - Root cause
    - Replacement hardware
    - Software patch
    - Redundancy
- Also referred to as Mean Time to Repair (MTTR)

#### Cost
- First question is often "How much will this be?"
- Initial installation costs
- Ongoing costs
- Replacement or repair costs
- Tax implications or depreciation
    - Handled by accounting

#### Responsiveness
- How fast do you get a response?
- Customers are very sensitive to this
- Everyone wants fast and immediate responses, especially for interactive applications
- Speed is the metric
    - Some functions may be fast
    - Some may be bottlenecks

#### Scalability
- How quickly and easily can we increase or decrease capacity
    - Might happen many times a day
    - Elasticity
- Always a resource challenge
- Needs to include security monitoring
    - More systems means more attack surfaces

#### Ease of Deployment
- Applications have many moving parts 
    - Servers, firewalls, etc
- It's an involved process
    - Hardware resources, cloud budgets, change control
- Might be very simple
    - Orchestration/automation
- Important to consider during product engineering phase

#### Risk Transference
- You can transfer risk to a third-party via insurance
- Cybersecurity insurance covers attacks and downtime
    - Popular thanks to ransomware
- Internal losses can be recouped 
- Does not stop cyberattacks, only gives you some money back on them
- Protects against legal issues from customers
    - Can help with legal proceedings from customers

#### Ease of Recovery
- Something will eventually go wrong
    - Time is money, how fast can you get back?
- Malware infection can be reloaded from original media, downtime can be up to an hour
    - Or you have a corporate image ready to be reloaded from, cutting it down to 10 minutes

#### Patch Availability
- Software isn't typically static, it has updates for bug fixes and security
- Oftentimes the first task after installation is checking if you're on the latest version
- Most companies have regular updates
- Some never patch, which is concerning

#### Inability to patch
- What if patching wasn't an option
- Legacy systems, embedded systems, RTOS, etc...
- Not designed for end-user updates
    - Very short sighted, especially nowadays
- May need additional security controls to keep attackers off

#### Power Infrastructure
- Electricity is the blood of computers
    - Requires extensive engineering
- Consider your requirements, are you an enterprise or a small business?
- You might want to consider backup services like generators or Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) in case primary power dies
    - Happens very often

#### Compute
- Does the heavy lifting for apps
- May be a single processor, easier to develop or thousands
- Cloud based compute engines can also be used
- Multiple CPUs over multiple clouds 
    - Adds complexity, but enhances scalability
