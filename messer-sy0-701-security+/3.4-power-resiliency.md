# 3.4 Power Resiliency

#### Power Resiliency
- Foundation of our technology
    - Important to properly engineer and plan for outages
- We usually don't make our own power
    - Usually provided by third-parties
- We can't control availability
    - So, we need ways to mitigate power issues both short and long term

#### UPS
- Uninterruptible Power Supply
    - Has batteries or power source to keep power during blackouts, brownouts, surges
    - Short-term backup power
- UPS types
    - Offline/Standby UPS are passive and only activate during power disruptions
    - Line-interactive UPS can slowly increase voltage when it sees a drop on the main power line, good for areas with frequent brownouts
    - On-line/Double-conversion UPS are always active and becomes the main power source, charged by the true main power source
- Features
    - Auto-shutdown when UPS drains, battery capacity, outlets, phone line suspension

#### Generators
- Long-term power backup
    - Fuel storage required, may be costly
- Can power an entire building
    - Some outlets may be marked as generator-powered
- May take a few minutes to get the generator running and usable
    - Use a battery UPS while generator is starting
